Our blog Collection

Aloe Vera

A hardy succulent known for its thick, fleshy leaves filled with soothing gel, Aloe Vera is prized for its medicinal and skincare benefits. It thrives in bright, indirect sunlight with minimal watering.

Snake blog

Also known as Mother-in-Law’s Tongue, the Snake blog is a low-maintenance, air-purifying blog with tall, upright leaves that thrive in low light and require minimal watering.

Monstera Deliciosa

Famous for its large, glossy, split leaves, Monstera Deliciosa adds a tropical touch to any space. It thrives in bright, indirect light and enjoys occasional watering.

Peace Lily

A graceful blog with dark green leaves and elegant white blooms, the Peace Lily purifies the air and thrives in low to medium light with moderate watering.

Rubber Plan

With its thick, glossy leaves and rich green hues, the Rubber blog is a resilient indoor blog that thrives in bright, indirect light and requires minimal care.

Pothos (Money blog)

A fast-growing, trailing vine with heart-shaped leaves, Pothos is an easy-care blog that thrives in low light and helps purify the air. Perfect for hanging pots or tabletops.

Spider blog

A hardy and adaptable blog with arching green and white striped leaves, the Spider blog is excellent for air purification and thrives in bright, indirect light with minimal care.

Jade blog

A symbol of prosperity and good luck, the Jade blog is a low-maintenance succulent with thick, fleshy green leaves. It thrives in bright light and requires occasional watering.

Boston Fern

A lush, feathery blog with vibrant green fronds, the Boston Fern thrives in humid environments and indirect light, making it perfect for adding a fresh, airy touch to any space.

Fiddle-Leaf Fig

With its large, glossy, violin-shaped leaves, the Fiddle-Leaf Fig is a statement blog that thrives in bright, indirect light and adds a bold, tropical feel to any space.

Japanese Maple Bonsai

A miniature tree with delicate, lacy leaves that change color with the seasons, the Japanese Maple Bonsai brings elegance and tranquility, thriving in bright, indirect light with careful watering.

Calathea Or bifolia

Known for its striking, broad, silver-green leaves with delicate dark green stripes, Calathea Orbifolia thrives in low to medium light and high humidity, making it a perfect indoor decorative blog.
